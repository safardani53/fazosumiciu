Zac Hanson welcomes his FIFTH child, son Quincy, with wife Kate and jokes that he's 'freaking out'

Publish date: 2024-06-22

ZAC Hanson has welcomed his fifth child, son Quincy Joseph Thoreau, with his wife Kate.

Zac, who is the youngest member of the '90s boyband Hanson, announced that their tot was born on Sunday and joked that he's "freaking out" about "raising another person."

The singer, 35, shared a black and white snap of him jokingly screaming in terror while cradling Quincy, as well as one of Kate holding the baby in hospital on Instagram on Tuesday.

He wrote: "I am a little late, but I celebrated #internationalwomensday the best way I know how.

"Marveling at the superpower women are uniquely gifted... as well as freaking out over the fact that I am now responsible to help raise another person."

Zac concluded: "Quincy Joseph Thoreau Hanson. Born 3/7/21."

Zac and Kate began dating in 2000 when they were teenagers and married in June 2006.

They are already parents to sons John Ira Shepherd, 12, George Abraham Walker, seven, and daughters Junia Rosa Ruth, 10, and Mary Lucille Diana, four.

Zac announced in January that Kate was pregnant, telling People: 'We're so happy and thankful for the things 2020 got right, especially this little guy coming in March!

"Here's to fresh starts and new beginnings."

Kathryn later told Us Weekly that they won't have any more kids after their fifth: "We feel really fulfilled and complete with five!

"The biggest difference this time around has been how much more aware and excited our kids are about the new baby now that they're older."

Zac previously shared a throwback of them posing at Disney World in 2002 as teenagers next to a snap of them in the same spot in 2018.

"Sixteen years later and even though so many things have changed, much remains the same #wannamakeout," he wrote.

The couple's baby news comes after Zac's older brother Taylor Hanson, 37, welcomed his seventh child in December with his wife Natalie.

His other brother, 40, has three children.

"It's a cool thing," Zac said of their children all growing up together, while appearing on the Lorraine talk show in February 2019.

"We spend a lot of time together touring, in the studio and around each other, so our kids know each other really, really well. They're really, really close."

Zac was six when he and his brothers formed Hanson in 1992, playing the drums and providing backing vocals.

Hanson's first single, MMMBop from their album Middle of Nowhere topped the Billboard Hot 100 chart, thus making Hanson one of the youngest artists to ever have a number one single.

At age 12, he was the second youngest person to be nominated for a Grammy award behind Michael Jackson.
