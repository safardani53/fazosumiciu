Jon Hamm, Jennifer Westfeldt: Why They Split

Publish date: 2024-09-25

Partners, but not parents. Jon Hamm and Jennifer Westfeldt announced that they were splitting after 18 years together on Sept. 7, and sources reveal exclusively to Us Weekly what exactly caused the breakup between the long-term couple.

One insider tells Us that the pair "definitely tried" to work out their differences and keep their nearly two-decade-long romance going. However, the split was imminent, especially since the two didn't see eye to eye about starting a family.

"There's always been a rift," another source close to the actress tells Us. "Because he wanted kids and she didn't."

Westfeldt, 45, opened up to the New York Times about the topic in more detail in 2012. "I never thought I'd be this age and not have kids," she reflected at the time. "But my life has also gone in a million ways I never anticipated. I kept feeling like I'd wake up with absolute clarity, and I haven't. And we have a pretty great life together… No one's had to give up an opportunity they really wanted."

In another interview with the Guardian, Westfeldt explained why marriage and kids weren't on her list of priorities. "In reality our lives — Jon's and mine — are so unconventional in every way, it's very difficult," she said that same year. "I think kids should have something grounded. Jon and I are both children of divorce."

Though the stars disagreed on the topic of kids, the nontraditional pair were always in agreement about their choice not to wed. "Neither of us have the greatest examples of marriages in our families," the Mad Men alum, 44, told Parade in 2010. "But Jen is the love of my life, and we've already been together four times longer than my parents were married."
