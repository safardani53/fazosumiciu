Popular pet food brand confirms fan-favorite is never coming back and customers are 'very upset'

Publish date: 2024-06-11

PET lovers have been dismayed to find out that their furry friend's favorite food has been pulled from shelves.

Popular pet food brand, Friskies, has confirmed via Twitter that its Glaz’d & Infuz’d with Chicken Gravy Wet Cat Food has been discontinued.

Previously, the company sold the product in most supermarkets, pet stores, and other retailers.

The wet cat food had grilled pieces of chicken in a thick gravy glaze mixed with garden veggies.

Other flavors from the Glaz’d & Infuz’d line included Tuna & Vegetable Gravy, Crab & Vegetable, and Shrimp Gravy.

After shopping for her feline friend, one buyer could no longer find the Friskies product anywhere and took to Twitter.

“Blue is very upset that your Glaz’d & Infuz’d chicken flavor has been out of stock for weeks. This baby likes nothing else, and she would like you to know about it,” she wrote.

The company quickly responded informing her that the particular product had been discontinued. 

In fact, after digging through the website, The U.S. Sun could not find any items from the Glaz’d & Infuz’d line.

While the company may no longer produce that line, it does have some similar items such as ​​the Extra Gravy Chunky Wet Cat Food with beef, chicken, salmon, or turkey.

A LITTLE HISTORY ON FRISKIES

Friskies, owned by parent company Purina, has been providing high-quality animal food since 1934.

The company originally started with dry dog food until 1956 when it started creating cat food, treats, and complements.

Today, Friskies focuses on keeping cats happy and healthy by offering all types of flavors cats love.

It has tons of wet and dry foods, treats and cat food complements.

The company is unique in that each recipe offers a balanced nutrition feeding your cat’s "playfulness and curiosity," according to the Friskies website.

Animal lovers aren't the only ones hurting, 90s kids are still mourning the loss of their favorite cereals.

For years, shoppers have been missing cereals like Mr. T Cereal, Hidden Treasures, Oreo's O's, and more.

Plus, Chicken & Waffles from Post was one of​​ several new cereal flavors the company put out in 2019.

A spin on Honey Bunches of Oats, the cereal had its iconic flakes and clusters but with maple-flavored, waffle-shaped, and drumstick-shaped pieces flavored with real poultry seasoning.

While the cereal was only created for National Cereal Day, it was short-lived much to fans’ dismay.

Another snack fans had to say goodbye to was the Deep Fried Twinkie.

While this snack has been around for years, the Deep Fried Twinkies by Hostess was popular after county fares fried the treat.

The company partnered with Walmart to distribute the frozen item that was first flash-fried.

The Deep Fried Twinkies could be eaten in under 10 minutes by way of the oven or by air fryer.
