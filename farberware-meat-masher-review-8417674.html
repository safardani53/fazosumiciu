The Farberware Meat Masher Is a Must-Have Kitchen Tool

Publish date: 2024-02-20

You know when you use a kitchen tool that’s so game-changing, you can’t believe you ever cooked without it? That just happened to me.

Last week, I hosted two holiday cocktail hours at my apartment and when it came to preparing appetizers, one tool was the star of the show: the Farberware Meat Masher. 

Every time I cook ground meat, whether it’s beef, turkey, chicken, or sausage, I curse myself for not owning a meat masher, which breaks up big chunks way better than any spatula or spoon can. So, when I decided to make a ground sausage appetizer for both parties, I finally bought the tool — and now I’m cursing myself for not snagging it sooner.

Farberware Meat Masher

This clever Farberware kitchen tool, which is only $10 on Amazon, can mash, mix, and chop a variety of foods. I used it to break up ground Italian sausage, and it worked faster than any other time I’ve cooked ground turkey for stuffed red peppers or ground beef for chili. Because the efficient tool mashes the meat into such small pieces, cooking time is slashed in half.

The handle is long enough that I didn’t worry about getting splashed by hot grease, and the five-blade design also doubles as a spatula to scrape remnants off the sides. The nylon material doesn’t scratch the bottom of my pan like a metal version might, and cleanup is a breeze (it’s also dishwasher safe.)
