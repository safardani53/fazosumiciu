“I had fought a lot with my Muslim identity,” says Mehmet Oz.

When questioned about his religion and identity in one of his interviews, Mehmet stated having a struggle with his Muslim identity. He explained how there are only two viewpoints in his family. His mother’s family, he claims, is highly secular. They take pride in the fact that they are secular. Mehmet explains how Islam is both a religion and a law. In Turkey, your political leaders are also your religious leaders, he explained. He also describes how difficult the parting was in Turkey.

Mehmet claims that his mother’s family is very religious but keeps it hidden from others. He also discussed how his father was able to get the top medical education in the world during the 1940s. According to him, his father’s family is likewise considerably more conventional. Religion was their most valuable asset because it provided them with morals. So, Mehmet, given the context, says that as a Turk reared in America with one father who had religious barriers and one who did not, he was drawn more to the spiritual element of religion rather than the legal side of religion.

Mehmet claims that in his mind, he used religion as a playing field since the laws of the game felt like the rules of the game. He describes it as a 100-yard-by-30-yard field with a hash mark every ten yards, which he claims is just a rule, but the actual game is where you can include the other human connection, which he defines as a spiritual connection that defines his religious belief and describes who he is and what his relationship with the god is.

Mehmet acknowledged that he discovered this reality not only in Islam, but also in his father’s family, who is from Nashville, Tennessee. Finally, he addressed the fact that although Sufi Islam covers the idea of religion brilliantly, Mehmet finds the legal side of religion bothersome.
