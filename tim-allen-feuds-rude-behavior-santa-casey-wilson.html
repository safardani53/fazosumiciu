Inside Tim Allen's feuds with co-stars as actor is accused of 'rude' on-set behavior by Santa Clause

Publish date: 2024-05-21

DIVE into comedian Tim Allen's feuds with his Home Improvement and Santa Clauses cast members. 

Actress Casey Wilson, 43, recently called out The Santa Clauses star over his rude on-set behavior. 

During her latest B***h Sesh podcast episode, the Happy Endings star opened up about her cameo appearance on the Disney+ show.

During the first episode, entitled Chapter One: Good to Ho, Santa (played by the Home Improvement star) revisited the home of the Little Girl (Casey) from the 1994 film. 

The little girl has grown up but has also forgotten about Santa.

Because she assumed Kris Kringle was a thief, the former little girl hilariously screamed and threw things at him.

'SINGLE WORST EXPERIENCE'

Casey did not find the experience funny and claimed: "Tim Allen was such a b***h.

"It was the truly single worst experience I’ve ever had with a co-star ever."

She continued: "So I’m in a scene. It’s just me and Tim Allen, and I’m supposed to throw things at him.

“I think he’s a burglar. So he’s coming down the chimney, obviously as Santa, and I am woken up thinking there’s an intruder, basically like a home invasion scene. So I’m throwing things at him."

Casey alleged: "He goes over to the producer, who is standing four feet from me, and goes, and I hear him, he goes: ‘You gotta tell her to stop stepping on my lines.’ 

"The producer turns to me with horror on his face and has to walk one foot to me, and he goes: ‘Um, Tim would ask that you stop stepping on his lines.’”

The Righteous Gemstones star concluded: "When he was done, he was so f***ing rude. 

"Never made eye contact, never said anything. It was so uncomfortable.”

Tim, 70, has not responded to Casey's claims. 

Casey is not the only person the Home Improvement star has feuded with. 

HOME IMPROVEMENT

During the '90s, the stand-up comic became known for Tim "Toolman" Taylor on the hit sitcom.

During the eight-season run, Tim and his wife Jill Taylor raise their three young boys - Mark (Taran Noah Smith), Randy (Jonathan Taylor Thomas), and (Brad Zachery Ty Bryan) - amid personal, financial, and familial crises.

In the show-within-the-show aspect, Pamela Anderson, 56, played Lisa the Tool Girl on Tool Time during the first two seasons. 

BEEF WITH PAMELA

In January 2023, the Baywatch actress claimed in her memoir, Love, Pamela, Tim flashed her on the ABC set. 

Pamela alleged: “On the first day of filming, I walked out of my dressing room, and Tim was in the hallway in his robe.

“He opened his robe and flashed me quickly — completely naked underneath.

She claimed: “He said it was only fair because he had seen me naked. Now we’re even. I laughed uncomfortably.” 

Tim denied Pamela's claims and told Variety: “No, it never happened. I would never do such a thing.”

THE FINAL SEASON

The Toy Story actor supposedly had beef with another co-star ahead of the show's final season.

 In 1999, Jonathan left the family comedy and reportedly wanted to concentrate on college, according to Page Six.

He did not reprise his role for the final episode. 

'MIFFED AT ME'

In May 1999, Tim claimed in an issue of TV Guide Magazine: “He was a little miffed at me.

"I was a little confused at why he didn’t want to do this whole year.

"He said it was about going to school, but then he did some films. Did he want to do films? Did he want to go to school?

Tim closed: “It got mixed up in the translation.

"I mentioned publicly that I was confused. I don’t think he liked that.”

BEEF SETTLED?

Jonathan and Tim supposedly patched things up as they reunited on the Fox sitcom, Last Man Standing.

In 2013, Jonathan played two characters: John Baker, a restaurant owner and boss of Kristin (Amanda Fuller), the eldest daughter of Mike Baxter (played by the Galaxy Quest star).

In March 2013, Tim praised and poked fun at his former co-star on Yahoo TV: "It was so much fun having him on the set, but he's there a lot, anyway.
