Oscars 2020: Little Womens Florence Pugh reveals she was TOPLESS in bed as she found out she was

Publish date: 2024-05-27

LITTLE Women star Florence Pugh was caught in a compromising position when her Oscars gong nomination arrived.

The English actress, 24, plays Amy March in the film adaptation of the iconic book, and has now been nominated for a top gong at the American ceremony.

Yet she took to Instagram to detail when her listing for Best Supporting Actress for the youngest March sister came through, she was actually stark naked.

As if to prove it, she uploaded before and after shots, presumably taken by a mate on the other side of the bed.

Florence, who found fame in an adaptation of Macbeth, added the caption: "Before the moment.. the exact moment."

She then posted: "#nominated #foraf**kingoscar!!!!!!" to cement her happiness.

The first snap saw her nattering away on the phone, blissfully unaware of the amazing news to come.

The second captured Florence screaming with happiness at her phone screen, while holding it facing her face.

The star even tagged two avocado Emoji icons across her chest to preserve her modesty as she lay in the crisp white sheets.

Fellow Hollywood A-Listers were quick to offer their congratulations, with High School Musical's Vanessa Hudgens posting: "Iconic."

DJ Edith Bowman added: "Amazing," while popstar Pixie Lott put two love hearts and the words: "OMG Yess so many congratulations."

Her award nomination is hot on the heels of her co-star Saoirse Ronan, who plays Jo Marsh, and is nominated for Best Actress at the star-studded ceremony.

Yet the movie's director, Greta Gerwig, was snubbed for the Best Director Award.

Meanwhile, there are a whopping 24 awards to be won at this year's Oscars.

Little Women is nominated for six gongs in total.

The Oscars will take place on February 9 at 11.30pm UK time and 6.30pm US time.

As usual, the glitzy ceremony will take place at the Dolby Theatre in the Hollywood and Highland Center in Los Angeles.

The show will be televised live in more than 25 countries and territories worldwide - including the UK.
