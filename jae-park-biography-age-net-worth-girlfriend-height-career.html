Jae Park is also known as Park Jae-Hyung, is a Korean-American musician, rapper, composer, and writer best known for his vocal and guitar roles in the South Korean rock band Day6. He rose to prominence after finishing as a finalist in the first season of the singing competition K-pop star.

Jae Park Birthdate, Height, and Weight

Jae Park was born in Buenos Aires, Argentina, on September 15, 1992. He is 31 years old and stands 5 feet 11 inches tall as of 2023. His weight is unknown. In 2017, he launched his own YouTube channel, Jaesix, which now has over a million subscribers.

Jae Park Educational qualifications

Jae Park attended Cerritos High School in Cerritos, California, and graduated with honors. He then enrolled at California State University to study Political Science, but he dropped out after being chosen for the Korean reality show K-Pop Star and relocated to South Korea. In 2017, he graduated as the After School Club’s MC.

Jae Park Career

Jae made his debut as a vocalist and electric guitarist in JYP’s first rock band, Day6, on September 7, 2015. The band made their debut with the EP The Day. Daydream, the band’s second EP, was released in late March 2016, and he was involved in the creation of two tracks: “Blood” and “First Time.” Following the release of their “Daydream” album, Jae continues to be involved in the creation of numerous tracks.

Jae joined Arirang’s After School Club as an MC on June 28, 2016, alongside Park Ji-min and Kevin Woo, formerly of U-KISS. On July 17, 2018, Jae received her diploma as ASC’s MC.

Every Monday, he was a guest DJ on Arirang Radio’s Monday Music Access, alongside singer Bernard Park. Jae’s last guest DJ appearance was on April 30, 2018.
