Publish date: 2024-03-04

Ziggy Ansah is a National Football League(NFL) professional football player. Moreover, he has served as a defensive end for many famous NFL teams.

The footballer’s career in the league started after the Detroit Lions drafted him in the first round. After six seasons with the Lion, he signed with the Seattle Seahawks for a year.

Later, he joined the San Francisco 49ers for the 2020 season. Currently, he is a free agent.

Besides that, he played collegiate football for Brigham Young University. He was an exceptional player for the BYU Cougars. 

Furthermore, Ziggy had no idea about American football until his youth. Correspondingly, he had only played soccer. 

Not to mention, he was more interested in basketball than other sports. However, when he landed in America, he had to change his interests and priorities. 

After playing collegiate football at BYU, he received well-deserved attention for his skills. Relying on the positive responses, he chose to enter the 2013 NFL Drafts, and the rest is history. 

The Ghanaian American player is one of the most hard-working players in the league. As a result, he was selected for the 2015 Pro Bowl.

Ziggy Ansah | Early Life, Family, And Education

Ziggy Ansah was born in Accra, Ghana. His parents are Edward Ansah and Elizabeth Ansah. 

Furthermore, the footballer’s Father was a sales manager for a petroleum Company. Meanwhile, his mother served as a nurse in a local hospital.

Besides that, Ezekiel has three brothers and a sister. His sister Elizabeth Gidding resides in London. 

In 2014, the Ghanaian American player surprised his sister during a London trip. He had not met her in over six years. For the first time, he meets his nephew since he was born.

The athlete had a pretty productive childhood. Moreover, he was quite into sports, and his parents always encouraged him to pursue them. 

The defensive end attended Golden Sunbeam Montessori School. As a school-going student in Ghana, he played soccer and basketball and ran tracks.

After that, Ziggy completed his high school education at Presbyterian Boys’ Senior Secondary School. He played almost all sports except for American football.

Ansah was particularly interested in basketball. He developed his skills while playing for the Forever Young Foundation’s turf. 

Likewise, he became a good friend of an LDS church missionary who visited his school. They kept in touch after the minister returned to the States. 

Later, the LDS church missionary encouraged him to play basketball at Brigham Youth University.

Eventually, he attended BYU but engaged with the football team rather than the basketball team.

Ziggy Ansah | High School And College Career

Initially, the NFL player attended Golden Sunbeam Montessori School, where he played basketball and various sports. With the school’s basketball team, he won multiple awards and honors.

Moreover, he even won the Most Valuable Player award. After that, Ziggy joined Presbyterian Boys’ Senior Secondary School to play high school basketball. 

Ansah was also on the track and field sports team. Following his high school graduation, the footballer served as a teaching assistant at Golden Sunbeam. 

Then he came to BYU with the assistance of Golden Sunbeam and mentored Alma Ohene Opare. She also helped the athlete settle and adjust in America. 

The team rejected him twice when Ezekiel applied for BYU’s basketball program. After much convincing, he tried out for BYU’s football program. 

To his surprise, the football team selected him. However, he had to learn the game from its very basics and even needed help to put on his pads. 

Nevertheless, in a short period, he became familiar with all the rules and techniques. Hence, he had an outstanding collegiate career after that.

Ziggy Ansah | NFL Career

Detroit Lions

Following his collegiate career, Ziggy entered the 2012 NFL Draft. In the beginning, his chances of being drafted were meager.

Nevertheless, he was an Outstanding Defensive Player in the 2013 Senior Bowl by the end of the year.

Shortly after, NFLDraftScout.com ranked Ansah the top defensive end and the seventh-best player.

Finally, the Lions drafted Ezekiel in the first round as the fifth pick overall. He also signed a four-year contract worth $18.59 million with the Detroit team. 

In his first season, he led the NFL rookies with eight sacks. Furthermore, the former Cougar received the Mel Farr Rookie of the Year honors. 

The following season, the Ghanaian American played in his first playoff game, where he tackled Tony Romo twice. Not to mention, the 49ers player was named to the Pro Bowl in 2015. 

After that, the defensive end made his first sack against Eli Manning in the 2016 season. However, a severe shoulder injury halted his flourishing career in 2018.
