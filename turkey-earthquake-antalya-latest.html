Turkey earthquake Antalya rocked by strong magnitude 5.2 earthquake with tourists saying buildin

Publish date: 2024-07-26

AN earthquake has struck in the Mediterranean with a magnitude of 5.2.

The quake hit just over 60 miles off the coast of Antalya, Turkey, just after 7.20 a.m. GMT.

Initial reports from the US Geological Survey (USGS) state the depth of the quake is around 36 miles, with tremors felt in surrounding areas.

Antalya is the country's fifth largest city and a popular destination for tourists.

It is renowned for its harbour, blue waters and long beaches, and is one of the most popular destinations for tourists visiting the country with around 9.6 million visiting the region this year.

One witness posted on the Last Quake website saying: "Very significant long lasting shaking - quite violent building shaking lasting a good time. Pretty scary!!"

Another, based 43 miles northwest of the quake's epicentre, wrote: "We felt the vibration and things were moving nothing fell down."

Concerned citizens have taken to Twitter to wish the best for the people in the area.

Yesterday the same area was rocked by a previous quake of 4.8 magnitude making this tremor the second in as many days.
