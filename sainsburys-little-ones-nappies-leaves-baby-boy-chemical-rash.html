Sainsbury's Little Ones nappies leaves baby boy in agony and with 'chemical' rash

Publish date: 2024-03-03

A BABY boy was left in agony after a "chemical reaction" to Sainsbury's nappies, his parents claim.

Ozzie Grant's legs, bottom and back became covered in blisters hours after his parents had put a nappy on.

They say the one-year-old began to associate being wiped with being in pain - clamping his legs together every time they tried to pick him up and screaming when they attempted to wipe the blistered areas.

Parents Albert Grant, 46, and Rebecca Robinson, 38, had purchased the 'Little Ones' nappies from a Sainsbury's branch in Blackpool, Lancs.

They claim the £3.50 pack of 30 nappies burnt his skin - and they now want the nappies to be pulled from the shelves.

The worried pair said doctors and the pharmacist they spoke to described the rash as a "chemical reaction" and prescribed an antihistamine cream and moisturiser.

Rebecca said: "We normally buy Pampers but it was a last minute decision. Within 20 minutes he was pulling at the nappy.

"By the next morning the redness was coming up his back and down his legs, within 12 hours he went from a blemish to a rash.

"When we tried to wipe him he was bleeding which may have been because the blisters had popped.

"When we tried to pick him up he clamped his legs together and we had to bath him every hour to cool down the blistered area.

"I've never seen anything like that on a child before.

"When he sees you get the baby wipes out he moves to the other side of the room, he now associates the wipes with pain which is awful."

The cause of the rash has not been confirmed but a Sainsbury's spokesman said they were investigating.

The couple took to the internet in the hope of discovering the cause of Ozzie's severe reaction and found a number of similar complaints.

A spokesman for Sainsbury's said: "This is obviously a really upsetting experience for Mr Grant and we hope his son feels much better soon.

"The safety and quality of our products is extremely important to us and we run dermatological testing to make sure they meet our high standards.

"We've sold millions of Little Ones nappies and, though it's extremely rare, babies can very occasionally have a reaction to a new brand of nappy.

"We will always investigate any concerns our customers share with us."
