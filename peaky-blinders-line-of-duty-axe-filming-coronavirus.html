Peaky Blinders and Line of Duty forced by coronavirus to axe filming as stars pull out of Graham Nor

Publish date: 2024-06-15


TWO of telly’s most popular shows were forced by the coronavirus to down tools on Monday.

Work had to stop on BBC dramas Line Of Duty and Peaky Blinders — and producers on The Graham Norton Show are struggling to find celebrities willing to appear.

The sixth series of police thriller Line Of Duty, starring Martin Compston, Adrian Dunbar and Vicky McClure, began filming in Northern Ireland last month — but is now postponed indefinitely.

It was the most-watched show on British TV last year, with more than 13million viewers tuning in.

The makers of period crime drama Peaky Blinders, which stars Cillian Murphy, have decided not to start filming until the industry is given the all-clear.

The BBC said the decision to suspend work on both series was “in consultation with and supported by” the corporation.

It added: “We will continue to review all productions on a case-by-case basis and to follow the ­latest advice from the Foreign Office, World Health Organization and Public Health England.”

The new series of The Graham Norton Show has been hammered by a lack of high-profile guests.

Producers say they do not know how to fill the sofa for the BBC1 chat show, due to resume in April.

It was set to return with a James Bond special. But that plan was wrecked when the release of new 007 movie No Time To Die was postponed due to the coronavirus.

With other Hollywood blockbusters cancelling releases, the supply of guests has dried up, putting the eight-week run under threat. The Friday night show would also have to be filmed without a live studio audience under new restrictions.

A source said: “One option is to get more UK-based TV stars.

"People like Michael Palin and Miriam Margolyes are always great at telling a story.

“But are there enough of those people to fill the whole series and will it work without an audience?”

James Corden’s Late Late Show has been suspended in the US, as has Ellen DeGeneres’s chat show.

The BBC is considering calling a halt to the Spring run of the ­Norton show altogether and replacing it with repeats and best-ofs. That would create a new problem for producers when the series returns in the Autumn.

The source added: “There’s going to be such a pile-up of blockbuster films that were postponed, we’re going to need a bigger sofa.”

The filming schedules of ITV soaps Coronation Street and Emmerdale are not yet disrupted.

Producers say the shows will use their platform to remind viewers of the importance of health precautions such as hand-washing.

Flu fighters

THE Foo Fighters have postponed their tour owing to coronavirus.

Frontman Dave Grohl, 51, told fans the US gigs next month would be rearranged. Grohl joked about the time he broke his leg in 2015 but still did gigs in a cast.

He said: “Hi, this is Dave. Remember me? The guy who wouldn’t postpone a show when my goddamn leg was falling off?

“Well playing a gig with a sock full of broken bones is one thing, but playing when your health and safety is in jeopardy is another.

“We f****** love you guys. So let’s do this right and rain check. Now go wash your hands.”

On Monday’s Coronation Street, viewers saw Geoff ask Yasmeen if she sang happy birthday twice while washing her hands. The scene was written last year and filmed in January, so despite its topicality was not originally intended as a virus reference.

ITV Studios’ John Whiston said: “Because we script and shoot so far in advance we don’t generally reflect contemporary issues.

“However we are going to use the soaps to remind people of important public health issues.”

A spokeswoman said producers were “confident” of being able to continue the filming schedule.

An unnamed Corrie cast member who went into self-isolation ten days ago after returning from a virus hotspot did not develop symptoms and has returned to work. On Monday some studio-based programmes, such as ITV’s Loose Women and Channel 5’s Jeremy Vine, went ahead without their usual live audiences.

The BBC’s celebrity version of Race Across The World has been postponed.

Handmaid’s Tale star Elisabeth Moss on Monday confirmed that work on series four of the show had halted. She said it would resume “as soon as it’s safe”.

A West End production of the play Endgame starring Harry Potter’s Daniel Radcliffe has also been cancelled. London’s Old Vic theatre said the decision was made “with great sadness.”

And Warner Bros confirmed filming of The Batman, starring Robert Pattinson, had been halted.
